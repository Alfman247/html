Basic Current Accounts

Basic Current Accounts are designed for those who would struggle to get other bank accounts, and therefore come with little-to-no benefits. Despite this, you still should not pick an account at random! To begin, I would highly reccomend in this scenario that you choose a bank with a branch near to you. Alternatively, if you already have an account such as a savings account with a bank, I would reccomend going with them. Standard Current Accounts

Now, the standard current account market is extremely saturated, as every bank and building society will offer you one. These usually have slightly stricter requirements than basic accounts, such as a minimun age of 18 instead if 16 and proof of address. Despite these the majority of the population of the UK will be able to get their hands on one. Another benefit of these accounts is that banks will occasionally offer special bonuses, such as nationwide recently offering cashback on a supermarket shop. This catagory also includes fintech banks, such as Starling Bank and Monzo, whi typically offer better apps than most banks, which may be important. However, due to their nature, these banks have no branches therefore meaning deposits must be made at the post office, which may be less convinient for you! To choose the correct account for you, I would reccomend looking at any benefits the bank will offer you, such as cashback from Chase or a better app from Starling. If nothing is standing out to you, I would reccomend following my previous advice and looking into nearby bank branches and customer satisfaction surveys. Due to this being my advice, I will not be placing another table.

However, this can come at a cost woth some bank accounts having montly fees as high as £24 a month! Most of these types of accounts will charge a monthly fee, however, some accounts will waive the fee if you have a certain amount in you account. Premium Current Accounts

The massive catch is the minimum income out of any of these accounts is £75,000. This is also known as a lot. If you are lucky enough to get your hands on one of these, you most certainly should as many of these banks will even provide you with luxurious benefits such as a concierge.
