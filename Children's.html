Children's

Prepaid vs Current

Prepaid or Current Account is a common question to ask when considering finance for your child. For the most part, I believe it depends on the child's age and financial confidence. This is due to the fact that most of the accounts that teach your child will often come with monthly fees and no interest. Alternatively to this, high street banks will offer you both no monthly a fee and a potentially decent interest rate, along with a JISA to help you save long-term for your child. I have included a table below to compare the features of a prepaid vs high street account.
Prepaid Accounts High-Street Acciunts
Usually a monthly fee Free
Educational tools built-in No educational features
No interest Quite good interest rate
Opened online Can be opened online or in-branch
From age six From around age 11 or 12

Saving Up!

It is important to teach your child the skill of saving up and a savings account is a great way of doing so. Most current accounts for children will come with a saver included and, as most are around the same interest rate, this is fine! However, if you are looking for the best possible interest rate, HSBC is the place to go. They always have a rate around the Bank of England interest rate and at the moment is even at 5%. Alternatively, you can go with the previously mentioned Premium Bonds as this is also a fairly great yet not gauranteed option.
